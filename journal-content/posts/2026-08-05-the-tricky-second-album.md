This post is the follow-up to my [Part 1 — Finding Your Next Product Role](/journal/2025-11-27-part-1-foundations-of-a-better-job-hunt)... the tricky second album.

## Fast, slow; slow fast

The hardest part of this is I found I had little control over the pacing. I was at the mercy of the hiring company or recruiter. When given dates for interviews I generally tried to find ones in the morning so I could get it over and done with but otherwise, if there was a gap, I just tried to enjoy the pause.

There was no logic to when new jobs appeared. They came like [London buses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bus_bunching) in the end; and fortunately the three that all came at once, two got to finals.

## Managing the emotional side

I look back at this and have no magic answer — there are a tonne of posts by other people who cover this far better than I ever would.[^1]

Looking for jobs is tough; people need support.

## What I'd do differently

This time, genuinely nothing. My process — or lack of process — worked for me. I appreciate it may not work for everyone. I've seen people set up some pretty sophisticated AI automation to help handle the processes. I elected not to and didn't need it to get a good result.

## Twists

One of the processes this time came back with two more final rounds (after passing the final round) — talk about never getting to enjoy the moment.

I've had this before on job hunts; for me it has never ended well. I still always play along but at the moment I have a 100% hit rate: when new rounds appear, I don't get the role. And this time was no different — maybe one day that will change, or maybe, just maybe… it's a sign that the job is just not right. Who knows.

Time for pastry. 🥐
